Biography

Bitch Magnet was an American post-hardcore band who formed in 1986 at Oberlin College in Ohio and later moved to North Carolina, United States. They released their first record in 1988. All of the band's albums were released on Communion Records in the US; they were also signed to the European labels Shigaku/What Goes On and Glitterhouse. The band disbanded in 1991.

Discography

Umber

Released: 1989
Genres: Post-Hardcore, Noise Rock
8/10

Bitch Magnet's first full album shows a wide variety of skill and sound. Songs like Motor and Navajo Ace show their hard rock side, while Clay and Big Pining show a balance between the soft and heavy sounds. Douglas Leader and Americruiser are two great displays of slow building post-rock-esque jams.

Highlights: Motor, Navajo Ace, Douglas Leader, Goat-Legged Country God, Joyless Street, Americruiser

Ben Hur

Released: 1990
Genres: Post-Hardcore, Noise Rock
7.5/10

The band's aggression is toned down a lot for this release, mainly using the vocals as a mere background, and focusing more on highlighting the noisy fuzz of their instrumental riffing. However this does not detract much from their consistency.

Highlights: Dragoon, Valmead, Mesentary, Gator, Crescent
