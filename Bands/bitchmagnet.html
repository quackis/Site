Biography

Bitch Magnet was an American post-hardcore band who formed in 1986 at Oberlin College in Ohio and later moved to North Carolina, United States. They released their first record in 1988. All of the band's albums were released on Communion Records in the US; they were also signed to the European labels Shigaku/What Goes On and Glitterhouse. The band disbanded in 1991.

Discography

Umber

Released: 1989
Genres: Post-Hardcore, Noise Rock
8/10

Bitch Magnet's first full album shows a wide variety of skill and sound. Songs like Motor and Navajo Ace show their mix of sludgy and noisy hard rock and early post-hardcore, while Clay and Big Pining show a balance between the buildup and breakdown of slow rhythms with more noisy riffs. Douglas Leader and Americruiser are two great displays of slow building post-rock-esque jams. Joan of Arc and Joyless Street are two straightforward, early post-hardcore inspired cuts. Punch and Judy is an emotional ride through angry vocals and a mix between angular and sludgy riffs.

Highlights: Motor, Navajo Ace, Douglas Leader, Goat-Legged Country God, Joyless Street, Punch and Judy, Americruiser

Ben Hur

Released: 1990
Genres: Post-Hardcore, Noise Rock
7.5/10

The band's aggression is toned down a lot for this release, mainly using the vocals as a mere background, and focusing more on highlighting the noisy fuzz of their instrumental riffing. However this does not detract much from their consistency. Dragoon is a collage of all their great sounds from Umber, built into an almost 10-minute epic. Mesentary presents Bitch Magnet's noisiest vocals in a fast moving track. Valmead and Crescent are more examples of how Bitch Magnet managed to craft a great balance of slow and fast rhythms.

Highlights: Dragoon, Valmead, Mesentary, Gator, Crescent
