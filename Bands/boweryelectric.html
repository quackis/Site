Biography

Bowery Electric was an American post-rock band, formed by Lawrence Chandler and Martha Schwendener in 1993.

Bowery Electric

Formed: 1993

Genres: Shoegaze, Trip Hop

Discography

Bowery Electric

Released: 1995
Genres: Shoegaze
6.5/10

The first release from Bowery Electric was an album filled with droning guitars, nonchalant and quiet vocals, and a sense of atmosphere. Sounds in Motion and the transition to Next to Nothing is a mesmerizing introduction. Slow Thrills is the loudest and densest track, as it develops a psychedelic trip. Deep Sky Objects is an almost upbeat rocker, keeping it's groove through mysterious vocals.

Highlights: Sounds in Motion, Next to Nothing, Deep Sky Objects, Slow Thrills, Out of Phase

Beat

Released: 1996
Genres: Shoegaze, Trip Hop
7.5/10

Bowery Electric's second effort, Beat, adds more elements to their equation. On top of their droning guitars are trip hop beats and dreamier vocals. This combination succeeds the most in the title track and Fear of Flying, where the tracks loom with mystifying sounds. Songs like Without Stopping and Inside Out have lengthy, repetitive effects. Coming Down and Empty Words are their shoegaze tracks, incorporating the hazy vocals and guitar fuzz. Postscript is a droning ambient piece that ebbs and flows through light intrumentation for 17 minutes.

Highlights: Beat, Empty Words, Fear of Flying, Black Light, Coming Down, Postscript
