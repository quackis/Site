Biography

Slint was an American rock band from Louisville, Kentucky, formed in 1986. The band consisted of guitarist and vocalist Brian McMahan, guitarist David Pajo, drummer and vocalist Britt Walford, Todd Brashear (bassist on Spiderland), and Ethan Buckler (bassist on Tweez).

Discography

Tweez

Released: 1989
Genres: Noise Rock, Post-Hardcore, Math Rock
8/10

Tweez, produced by Steve Albini, showcases a band that's already comfortable with their sound. Ron and Carol are both heavy and abrasive boogies, revealing both spoken word and piercing shrieks. Nan Ding is an eerie spoken word over a repetitive guitar riff. Kent is lonely and meandering trip. Charlotte and Warren reveal more of the faster and industrial sounds they're capable of. Darlene is a spoken word whisper. Pat is a fast and rhythmic tune which struggles to stand still. Rhoda close the album with a piercing guitar instrumental.

Highlights: Ron, Carol, Kent, Charlotte, Darlene, Pat

Spiderland

Released: 1991
Genres: Post-Hardcore, Post-Rock
10/10

Spiderland is an ultimate masterpiece of rock music. The six tracks of Spiderland all deconstruct and revolutionize many of the sounds of rock before them. Breadcrumb Trail is a spoken word track that guides the listener through a story of a fortune teller at a carnival. The track leads up to multiple climaxes of screams with fuzzy and noisy instrumentals to back it up. Nosferatu Man presents the story of Nosferatu behind a slow and repetitive rhythm. Don, Aman tells the dark story of a suicidal man at a party, with the story enhanced through the spoken word style. The song reaches a peak of noise near the end until it delves into a slow outro. Washer is a slow and psychedelic song, reaching another noisy peak with loud jarring guitars, until it once again falls into a slow outro. For Dinner... is an almost peaceful instrumental, playing with slow paces and silent passages. Good Morning Captain, which is most likely their greatest song, tells the story of a captain at sea using the same spoken word process. The instrumental guides the voice of the narrator through multiple highs and lows, until the song and album reaches its' catharsis, with the captain screaming at the top of his lungs to close out the album.

Highlights: The full album
