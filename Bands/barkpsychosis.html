Biography

Bark Psychosis are an English post-rock band/musical project from east London formed in 1986. They were one of the bands that Simon Reynolds cited when coining "post-rock" as a musical style in 1994, and are thus considered one of the key bands defining the genre.

Bark Psychosis

Formed: 1986

Genres: Post-Rock

Discography

Hex

Released: 1994
Genres: Post-Rock
8/10

The debut album from Bark Psychosis was one of the pioneering works of the 90's post-rock uprising. Utilizing a wide variety of instruments, in sync with soft and calming songs developed a blissful combination. The seven tracks of Hex emphasize the calming nature of music, never pushing into over the top rhythms and sounds, but instead slowly building and flowing through each piece, almost aimlessly.

Highlights: The Loom, A Street Scene, Absent Friend, Eyes and Smiles, Pendulum Man

Independency (EP)

Released: 1994
Genres: Post-Rock
7.5/10

Independency is a compilation of Bark Psychosis's pre-Hex EPs and singles. Each song is powerful in it's own way, and none of them settle to be too standard or similar. Although the release lacks the same pacing as Hex would master, it is still undoubtedly a solid release with very few weak points. The two EPs, All Different Things and Manman are the most consistent, and Scum may be one of the greatest works of the 90s.

Highlights: All Different Things, By Blow, Manman, Blood Rush, Tooled Up, Scum

///Codename: Dustsucker

Released: 2004
Genres: Post-Rock
8/10

Where Hex succeeds in the soft and bare, minimilast approach to rock, Bark Psychosis's second album after a decade shows the group turning to a more ethereal and hypnotic approach. Graham Sutton's skill in developing mesmerizing instrumentals proves that even a change in style doesn't prevent him from exceeding in what he already did so well. ///Codename: Dustsucker is an impressive release considering the decade between albums.

Highlights: From What Is Said to When It's Read, The Black Meat, Miss Abuse, 400 Winters, Shapeshifting, Rose
