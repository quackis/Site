Discography

Head

Released: 1990
Genres: Noise Rock, Post-Hardcore
7/10

First full album, produced by Steve Albini and released through Touch and Go records. Their sound is loud and chaotic, with aggressive and grating instrumentals.

Highlights: One Evening, My Own Urine, If You Had Lips, 7 vs. 8, Waxeater, Killer McHann

Goat

Released: 1991
Genres: Noise Rock, Post-Hardcore
8.5/10

The Jesus Lizard's second release is their masterpiece. Noisier and more grating guitar riffs, pounding drums and dizzying cymbals, and prophetic chanting vocals. From start to finish Goat is an endless barrage of psychotic ramblings and noisy instrumentals, with almost no breathing room in between.

Highlights: The full album

Liar

Released: 1992
Genres: Noise Rock, Post-Hardcore
8/10

A logical continuation of the sounds and synergy portrayed on Goat. At some points crazier, at some louder, but much of what is seen on Goat is brought over to Liar. Some of the highlights of Liar might be greater to some than that of Goat.

Highlights: Boilermaker, Gladiator, Slave Ship, Puss, Whirl, Zachariah, Dancing Naked Ladies

Down

Released: 1994
Genres: Noise Rock, Post-Hardcore
7.5/10

The Jesus Lizard's most polished release yet, which is saying a lot considering their records before. Nonetheless, it's all the greatness they've already perfected from Goat and Liar now more refined. While not much new is brough to the table, they see no need to change from their insanity.

Highlights: Fly on the Wall, Queen for a Day, The Associate, Destroy Before Reading, Horse, The Best Parts

Shot

Released: 1996
Genres: Noise Rock, Post-Hardcore
7/10

By this time much of their sound has been exhausted, however they still prove to be consistent and confident. Shot is still the same Jesus Lizard, however this is the first of their album's to lack Steve Albini's production, which does bring a new element to David Yow's yelling.

Highlights: Thumper, Blue Shot, Thumbscrews, Skull of a German, Trephination, Too Bad About the Fire, Inamorata

Blue

Released: 1998
Genres: Noise Rock, Post-Hardcore
6.5/10

The Jesus Lizard walk the line between their own deranged noise rock and a much more tame alternative rock on Blue. However, that isn't to say they've lost their charm. At some points it works and others it doesn't, leaving The Jesus Lizard's last album to be a good point to end, sooner than later.

Highlights: I Can Learn, Horse Doctor Man, Eucalyptus, A Tale of Two Women, Until It Stopped to Die, Needles for Teeth
