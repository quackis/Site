The Bedlam in Goliath

Released: 2008
Genres: Progressive Rock
7.5/10

The Bedlam in Goliath continues The Mars Volta's signature progressive sound. In some forms this is their most hectic album yet, with songs like Metatron and Goliath never seeming to flow fluidly. However, while this album does master the chaotic boogies we know so well, it does not reach the highs of the previous two albums, nor does it present the same cohesion. Nonetheless, this album is still the classic The Mars Volta.

Frances the Mute

Released: 2005
Genres: Progressive Rock
10/10

Frances the Mute not only improves upon the elements of De-Loused, it presents a multi-layered story that delves into a variety of genres. Each song is a masterful piece, and in cohesion they work even better together. From the magnificent build up and jam of Cygnus...Vismund Cygnus to the haunting and beautiful The Widow. From the psychedelic and latin covered L'Via L'Viaquez to the eery and disturbing Miranda That Ghost Just Isn't Holy Anymore. To finally reach the climax at the 32 minute epic Cassandra Gemini, which highlights all of what has been shown on the album thus far. This album is a tour de force on all levels, and is truly one of great works of progressive rock.
