Biography

Bluetile Lounge is an Australian slowcore band, formed in Perth, Western Australia in 1991. After releasing their debut full-length album, Lowercase, in 1995, Bluetile Lounge signed to the American independent label Smells Like Records, which released the band's second LP, Half-Cut, in 1998.

Bluetile Lounge

Formed: 1991

Genres: Slowcore

Discography

Lowercase

Released: 1995
Genres: Slowcore
7/10

Bluetile Lounge's debut album was released amongst the few pivotal slowcore groups of the 90's, including Codeine, Red House Painters, and Low. Bluetile Lounge found a union of melancholic instumentals and sleepylike vocals, displayed through five extended tracks. Ambered is the highlight of Lowercase, turning a drowsy lullabyesque piece into a gorgeous synthesis of melodies. Wriding is the densest and most atmospheric track, while Gm and Figure Ground work as lush instrumentals that carry the soft vocals along with it. The Weight (and the Sea) builds another drifting song into a collective fusion before ending.

Highlights: Wriding, The Weight (and the Sea), Ambered

Half-Cut

Released: 1998
Genres: Slowcore
7.5/10

The second and final album before Bluetile Lounge's split, Half-Cut, improves and elevates the sounds they displayed on their debut album. Liner is a fuzzy, shoegazey intro that transitions back and forth between a quiet and loud slowcore melody. Steeped and Lapsis are two peaceful songs, slowly leading to calming climaxes. Ltd reintroduces the fuzzy guitars of Liner, in combination with some of their most emotional vocals of the album. Old Star might be the masterpiece of Half-Cut, leading a four minute sleepy intro into a powerful and dense rhythm, to once again fade and then reanimate into a powerful ending.

Highlights: Liner, Steeped, Lapsis, Ltd, Old Star
