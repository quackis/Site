Biography

Bauhaus is an English rock band, formed in Northampton, England, in 1978. The group consists of Daniel Ash (guitar, saxophone), Peter Murphy (vocals, occasional instruments), Kevin Haskins (drums) and David J (bass). One of the pioneers of gothic rock, Bauhaus were known for their dark image and gloomy sound, although they mixed many genres, including dub, glam rock, psychedelia, and funk.

Discography

In the Flat Field

Released: 1980
Genres: Post-Punk, Gothic Rock
7.5/10

Following the two singles Bela Lugosi's Dead and Dark Entries, which the former might be their masterpiece and the latter an addictive post-punk anthem, Bauhaus released their debut album. While it may have not lived up to the two singles they prefaced the album with, they still managed to create a consistent and impressive first album. The lead combination of Double Dare and In the Flat Field is hypnotizing. Songs like A God in an Alcove and The Spy in the Cab set the stage for much of the 80s gothic sound. The finale of Nerves is a fantastic end to the album.

Highlights: Double Dare, In the Flat Field, A God in an Alcove, The Spy in the Cab, Stigmata Martyr, Nerves
