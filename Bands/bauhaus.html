Biography

Bauhaus is an English rock band, formed in Northampton, England, in 1978. The group consists of Daniel Ash (guitar, saxophone), Peter Murphy (vocals, occasional instruments), Kevin Haskins (drums) and David J (bass). One of the pioneers of gothic rock, Bauhaus were known for their dark image and gloomy sound, although they mixed many genres, including dub, glam rock, psychedelia, and funk.

Discography

In the Flat Field

Released: 1980
Genres: Post-Punk, Gothic Rock
7/10

Following the two singles Bela Lugosi's Dead and Dark Entries, which the former might be their masterpiece and the latter an addictive post-punk anthem, Bauhaus released their debut album. While it may have not lived up to the two singles they prefaced the album with, they still managed to create a consistent and impressive first album. The lead combination of Double Dare and In the Flat Field is hypnotizing. Songs like A God in an Alcove and The Spy in the Cab set the stage for much of the 80's gothic sound. The finale of Nerves is a fantastic end to the album.

Highlights: Double Dare, In the Flat Field, A God in an Alcove, The Spy in the Cab, Stigmata Martyr, Nerves

Mask

Released: 1981
Genres: Post-Punk, Gothic Rock
7.5/10

Bauhaus's second full album, released a year after In the Flat Field, represents a great progression for their sound. Instead of repeating the gothic essence of their first album, the addition of keyboards, acoustic guitar, and the saxophone add more layers to their dark style. It seemed to also let them experiment more, working much in their favor. Hair of the Dog might be their most punk song on the album, where The Passion of Lovers is a hard rocking chant. Of Lillies and Remains and Muscle in Plastic are dub-tinged bass driven tracks. Dancing and The Man with X-Ray Eyes are two great gothic additions. Kick in the Eye is a dance-punk jig, and shows Bauhaus at their catchiest. Mask is a dark and eerie conclusion to the album, ending with a loud and angular guitar rhythm.

Highlights: Hair of the Dog, Passion of Lovers, Of Lillies and Remains, Dancing, Kick in the Eye, The Man with X-Ray Eyes, Mask

The Sky's Gone Out

Released: 1982
Genres: Post-Punk, Gothic Rock
6/10

The Sky's Gone Out starts with a cover of Brian Eno's incredible Third Uncle, and while the cover isn't bad, it's not the strongest start to the album. Silent Hedges and In the Night are redeeming follow up songs, however the six minute Swing the Heartache doesn't lead anywhere. Spirit leads a peaceful buildup into a frenzied chant. The three parts of The Three Shadows meander without much of interest. All We Ever Wanted Was Everything is a sad ballad. Exquisite Corpse is an almost awkward piece, that builds to an aggressive freakout, to then delve into the sound of someone sleeping, to once again retreat into it's aggression.

Highlights: Silent Hedges, In the Night, Spirit, All We Ever Wanted Was Everything, Exquisite Corpse

Burning From the Inside

Released: 1983
Genres: Post-Punk, Gothic Rock
6.5/10

Much of the making of this album took place during internal difficulties with the band members, which on listen is almost hard not to hear. The continuity and focus of the group seemed to have been at an all time low. Still, Bauhaus was able to craft some notable tracks including the manic Antonin Artaud, and the slow and gorgeous Who Killed Mr. Moonlight. There's also Bauhaus's longest song since Bela Lugosi's Dead, Burning From the Inside which is a minimal post-punk experiment, toying with echoing vocals and an endless bass groove. Hope ends the album on an oddly light note.

Highlights: She's in Parties, Antonin Artaud, Who Killed Mr. Moonlight, Slice of Life, Burning From the Inside

Go Away White

Released: 2008
Genres: Post-Punk, Gothic Rock
4.5/10

Bauhaus's fifth album, coming 25 years after their previous effort, fails to revive the remaining energy they had in the 80's. The tracks seem to work too hard to recreate old sounds, none of which sticks. Endless Summer Of The Damned might be the only song to sound natural in it's endeavor, and could easily fit in with some of their early albums. Mirror Remains is an attempt to rehash their slow and eerie tracks such as Haunting Hills or Mask. The Dog's A Vapour is another creepy, slow burning track that ends in a psychotic and industrial soundscape.

Highlights: Endless Summer Of The Damned, Mirror Remains, The Dog's A Vapour
